# Retail-Store-Sales-Analysis

This project analyzes sales data for a retail store, exploring item demand, customer spending patterns, and category performance.

## Dataset

The dataset includes:
- **Item Name**: Name of the product
- **Category**: Product category
- **Price Per Unit**: Price of each item
- **Quantity**: Number of items sold
- **Total Spent**: Total spending per transaction
- **Customer ID**: Unique identifier for each customer
- **Sales Channel**: In-store or online
- **Payment Method**: Cash, card, etc.
- **Discount Applied**: Whether a discount was applied

## Analysis

The analysis covers:
1. Distribution of sales by **category** and **item**
2. Quantity sold per **price point**
3. Customer spending patterns
4. Sales channels, discounts, and payment methods

## Key Insights

1. **No correlation between price and quantity sold**, indicating high demand across all items.  
2. **Average spending per order**: $130  
3. **Most purchased item**: Item 2 from Beverages at $6.50 per piece  
4. **Sales per category** are almost equal (~8,000 pieces per category)  
5. **Customer spending extremes**:  
   - Highest: `CUST_24` → $68,452  
   - Lowest: `CUST_25` → $57,155.50  
   - Average: $62,056.20 per customer  
6. **Sales channels**: No significant variation between in-store and online purchases  
7. **Discounts**: Most customers did not receive a discount  
8. **Payment method**: Cash is most common, though not by a large margin

## Actionable Recommendations

- Focus inventory on high-demand items (e.g., Item 2 - Beverages).  
- Consider targeted promotions for slower-moving items.  
- Maintain balanced stock across all categories.  
- Reward top customers with loyalty programs.  
- Monitor payment methods and sales channels for operational efficiency.
